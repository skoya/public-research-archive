Coinbase Prime — Ops & Controls Framework

Approval workflows · YubiKey lifecycle · Human processes · On-chain transfer protection · Risk register · Pre-custody scenario library

01

Executive Summary

This framework defines the minimum operational controls required before placing institutional assets into Coinbase Prime custody. It covers the end-to-end human processes, technology controls, YubiKey hardware token lifecycle, on-chain transfer protections, residual risk register, and a mandatory scenario library that must be executed and signed off before go-live. Target audience: Operations, Information Security, Risk Management, and Legal & Compliance.

Coinbase Prime provides strong platform-level controls — role-based access separation, multi-party consensus policies, FIDO2 hardware MFA, and video verification for large transfers. However, the platform cannot protect against process failures on the client side: an insider colluding with a colleague, an approver who approves without checking the destination, a poorly configured quorum that can be met by two people in the same office, or assets placed into custody before the team has been trained and tested. This framework fills that gap.

The assumed hardware baseline is a company-issued, locked-down Microsoft Surface Laptop running Windows 11 Enterprise, used exclusively for Coinbase Prime approvals and managed via MDM. YubiKey 5C NFC hardware tokens are the MFA device for all approver accounts. No approval may be performed from a personal device, a shared workstation, or a general-purpose laptop.

All 12 scenarios in Section 9 must be executed and evidence retained before any real assets are placed in custody. This is a hard gate, not a recommendation.

Scope note: This framework covers operational controls on the client (institution) side. It does not replace Coinbase Prime's own terms, compliance obligations, or security documentation. Always verify current platform behaviour against Coinbase Prime's official help centre and your account team.

YubiKey Lifecycle Management

Coinbase Prime requires FIDO2/WebAuthn hardware security keys for approver authentication. The recommended device is the YubiKey 5C NFC (USB-C connector for Surface Laptop + NFC as backup). Each approver must have two keys: a primary key kept on their person and a backup key stored in a separate, access-controlled location.

Lifecycle Stages

StageResponsible PartyControlsEvidence Required
1. Procurement Security Officer Order direct from yubico.com only. Never use third-party resellers — supply chain integrity cannot be verified. Order 2 × YubiKey 5C NFC per approver. Verify tamper-evident packaging on receipt. Log serial numbers before distribution. Purchase order + delivery receipt + serial number log
2. Registration Each Approver (self-service) Each approver must self-register both their primary and backup key against their own Coinbase Prime account. An Administrator cannot pre-register keys on behalf of another user — Coinbase requires the account owner to be authenticated before adding a security key. Registration must occur on the approved Surface Laptop, on the corporate network, with Security Officer present. Coinbase account activity log screenshot showing key registration event + date/time
3. Distribution Security Officer + IT Primary key: handed to approver in person with ID check, witnessed and signed. Backup key: placed in company safe or secure cabinet under dual-control (two keyholders required to access). Never deliver primary and backup keys in the same package or hand-off session. Signed distribution log (approver, witness, date, serial numbers)
4. Storage Approver (primary) + Security Officer (backup) Primary: on approver's person or in their locked desk safe during working hours. Backup: company safe at a different physical location from primary (different floor or building preferred). Serial number of each stored key visible on the container label. Quarterly physical inventory check — Security Officer verifies both keys per approver are accounted for
5. Annual Rotation Security Officer All keys rotated annually at minimum. Early rotation triggered by: role change, suspected compromise, employment termination, or failed physical inventory. New keys registered before old keys revoked — no gap in approver coverage. Rotation log (date, approver, reason, new serial numbers)
6. Offboarding IT + Security Officer + People team Coinbase Prime account deactivated by Administrator before device and key handover. IT collects both keys on last day of employment. Keys destroyed per NIST 800-88 (physical destruction preferred for hardware tokens; document destruction method). Quorum check performed — confirm remaining approvers still meet MAJORITY threshold. Signed offboarding checklist (IT, Security, manager). Destruction certificate. Updated quorum calculation.
7. Lost Key Recovery Security Officer Immediate escalation to Security Officer. Approver temporarily suspended from quorum pending key replacement (recalculate remaining quorum). Security Officer contacts Coinbase Prime support to flag the account. IT opens company safe (dual-control) to retrieve backup key. Approver activates backup as primary. New physical key ordered from Yubico. Incident report filed. Incident report. Time-stamped escalation log. Target SLA: backup operational within 4 business hours; new primary ordered within 2 business days.
Supply chain warning: There have been documented cases of counterfeit and tampered YubiKeys sold via third-party marketplaces (Amazon Marketplace, eBay). Always order direct from yubico.com and verify the tamper-evident hologram and packaging on receipt. A compromised key from the factory could bypass MFA entirely.

All steps from Workflow A apply, plus:

  1. Security Officer is notified in advance of the transfer submission (minimum 2 hours notice where possible)
  2. Lead Approver calls the Initiator on a recorded line before approving — verbally confirms: amount, destination counterparty name (not just address), and business purpose

Geographic Distribution Requirements

At minimum, one Transfer Approver must be located in a different physical office or country from the other approvers. This provides:

  • Operational resilience — a local incident (fire, power outage, evacuation) cannot simultaneously prevent all approvals
  • Jurisdiction diversification — a single legal hold order in one jurisdiction cannot freeze the entire quorum
  • Coercion resistance — physical coercion of one group of approvers in the same location cannot meet the threshold alone

On-Chain Transfer Protection

On-chain transfers carry the highest residual risk of any operation on the platform because they are irreversible. Once a transaction is broadcast to the blockchain, there is no recall mechanism, no chargeback, and no recourse except pursuing the recipient through legal channels. The controls in this section must be treated as mandatory, not advisory.

Address Verification Protocol

Before adding or using any on-chain address, the verifier must:

  1. Receive the address via the primary channel (e.g. secure form, system of record)
  2. Receive the same address via a second independent channel (e.g. Signal from the counterparty, printed document, or contract PDF) — NOT email, NOT a forwarded message
  3. Compare the addresses character by character at minimum: first 6 characters, last 6 characters, and 3 random positions from the middle
  4. Confirm the network/chain matches (BTC address for a BTC transfer, ERC-20 for ETH network, etc.)
  5. Have a second person perform the same independent comparison and sign off in the address log
Address poisoning: Attackers monitor blockchain transaction history and generate vanity addresses matching the first and last few characters of frequently-used addresses. They send dust transactions to seed the poisoned address in the target's history. Always use the character-by-character check — never copy an address from transaction history.

VAULT Wallet Policy

  • All holdings above $100,000 must be in VAULT wallets, not TRADING wallets
  • VAULT withdrawals require a separate Activity approval for every withdrawal — they cannot be automated via API without explicit human approval in the loop
  • VAULT wallet creation itself requires an Activity approval — it is not instantaneous
  • Never use TRADING wallets for long-term custody of significant assets — TRADING wallets have lower approval friction

Gaps & Recommendations

The following are not provided by the Coinbase Prime platform — they must be built and maintained by the institution.

1. Out-of-Band Communication Channel

Coinbase Prime notifies approvers via platform email. Email is not a secure real-time channel for transfer confirmation — it is susceptible to phishing, forwarding, and delay. The institution must establish a dedicated out-of-band channel (Signal, encrypted corporate messaging, or a dedicated SMS service) used exclusively for transfer confirmation and YubiKey recovery coordination. Never use WhatsApp, standard SMS, or consumer email for transfer confirmation.

2. Incident Response Playbook

Coinbase has a support contact (primeops@coinbase.com) but does not provide a client-specific incident response runbook. The institution must build one covering: who to call at Coinbase and with what SLA, internal escalation chain (Security Officer → CISO → CEO), time-to-containment targets, post-incident review process, and how to handle a situation where a transfer has already executed and may be fraudulent (blockchain analytics, law enforcement, legal hold on Coinbase side).

3. Insurance Coverage Verification

Coinbase Prime holds its own crime and cyber insurance, but this covers Coinbase's liabilities, not necessarily client asset losses. The institution must verify with its own insurer: does the crime policy cover theft by a colluding employee using valid credentials? Does the cyber policy cover a social engineering attack that resulted in an approved (but fraudulent) transfer? What is the notification window? These are not abstract questions — they determine whether losses are recoverable.

4. Tabletop Exercise (Pre-Go-Live)

Before placing real assets in custody, run a tabletop exercise with all stakeholders simulating a realistic compromise scenario — for example: "An approver reports their Surface Laptop missing. A pending $2M withdrawal Activity was submitted 6 hours ago by the Initiator. What do we do?" Work through the responses in real time. Surface gaps in the IR playbook before they occur in a live incident. Document findings and close all gaps before go-live.

5. Annual Access Recertification

Coinbase Prime does not automatically prompt access reviews. The institution must build a quarterly or annual review process: all roles confirmed with their managers, YubiKey physical inventory checked and signed, API keys audited for continued necessity and scope, org chart segregation re-verified (no new reporting-line conflicts), and the scenario library re-run for any new Approvers onboarded since the last review.

6. Penetration Testing

Engage a specialist firm to test the approval Surface environment and the network segment. Specific focus areas: can an attacker on the general corporate network reach the approval VLAN? Can the USB device policy be bypassed by a creative attacker (e.g. a USB hub with a hidden HID device)? Is the EDR detectable or disableable by a sophisticated adversary? Test at least annually and after any significant infrastructure change.

7. Regulatory Notification Obligations

In most jurisdictions, a fraudulent crypto transfer must be reported to financial regulators (FCA, FinCEN, etc.) within a defined window — sometimes as short as 24–72 hours. This requirement must be pre-drafted in the IR playbook so the team knows exactly what to do and who to notify during an incident, not afterwards. Engaging legal counsel to produce a jurisdiction-specific matrix is strongly recommended before go-live.

8. Coinbase Prime as Single Point of Failure

If Coinbase Prime is unavailable (platform outage, regulatory action, or insolvency), access to custodied assets may be limited or delayed. The institution should understand: what are Coinbase's withdrawal SLAs during an outage? What is the segregation of client assets from Coinbase's own balance sheet (are client assets truly segregated)? Is there a path to move assets to a different custodian if needed, and how long does it take? These questions should be answered in the business continuity plan before go-live.

Summary: Coinbase Prime provides an institutional-grade approval and access control framework. The gaps above are not platform failures — they are the client-side responsibilities that no custodian platform can fulfill on your behalf. Address all 8 points before placing significant assets in custody.
